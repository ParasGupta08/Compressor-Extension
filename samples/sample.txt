File compression is the process of reducing the size of one or more files so they occupy less storage space.
Compression algorithms exploit patterns, repetitions, and statistically predictable sequences in data.
Lossless compression allows perfect reconstruction: ZIP, PNG, FLAC.
Lossy compression discards imperceptible data: JPEG, MP3, MP4.

Shannon entropy measures the theoretical lower bound on compression.
Run-Length Encoding replaces consecutive repeated values with count+value pairs.
Dictionary-based methods like LZ77 replace recurring patterns with back-references.
Huffman coding assigns shorter codes to more frequent symbols.
